The Zettelkasten Manual

A personal knowledge organizing system that lasts for a lifetime.

Zettelkasten is a non-linear note filling system used by Niklas Luhmann, which resulted in a communication partner or so-called "second brain" of him.

While it's very close to a personal wiki in form, but how these two are created and what they are capable of are fundamentally different. Simply put, a Zettelkasten might be implemented by a wiki, but a wiki doesn't have to be a Zettelkasten, most of them are not.

This manual is an outcome of years of Zettelkasten practice by the author.

Common Zettelkasten Misconceptions

  • Do I need a specialized application?

    You'd better not, not just because of vendor locked-in, the method works independently of any specific tool. Most digital Zettelkasten tools miss the fundamental concepts, for example, using a long timestamp as the ID of a note.

  • There's a "correct" way to implement Zettelkasten that works for everyone?

    No, there's no single "correct" way to implement Zettelkasten—by understanding the core ideas (and why), you can develop your own approach to implementing the Zettelkasten method that works for you the best.

  • Zettelkasten is a complex System to Learn?

    No, unlike some other resources, The Zettelkasten Manual is not intentionally complicated. It aims to be as direct and simplified as possible. The sophistication of the Zettelkasten comes from organic knowledge growth, not the complexity of the method itself.
